Kowloon - Scenic Spots

🛳️ Star Ferry

A timeless icon of Hong Kong, the Star Ferry has been crossing Victoria Harbour since 1888, connecting Tsim Sha Tsui in Kowloon with Central and Wan Chai on Hong Kong Island. More than just a mode of transport, it’s a nostalgic voyage through the city’s maritime heritage. The green-and-white double-decker ferries offer unbeatable views of the skyline for just a few Hong Kong dollars.

By day, the ferry glides past gleaming towers and bustling piers. By night, it becomes a front-row seat to the Symphony of Lights, a dazzling multimedia show that illuminates the harbor. It’s a short ride—just 10 minutes—but one that captures the soul of the city.

🛍️ Temple Street Night Market

When the sun sets, Temple Street comes alive with neon lights, sizzling street food, and the buzz of bargaining. Located in Yau Ma Tei, this iconic night market is a sensory overload in the best way. Stalls brim with souvenirs, electronics, jade trinkets, and vintage curios.

But it’s not just about shopping—fortune tellers, Cantonese opera singers, and open-air seafood stalls turn the street into a stage. Grab a bowl of claypot rice or spicy curry fish balls, and soak in the electric atmosphere. It’s gritty, authentic, and utterly unforgettable.

🌿 Nan Lian Garden

Step into Nan Lian Garden and you’ll feel like you’ve wandered into a Tang Dynasty painting. This 3.5-hectare classical Chinese garden in Diamond Hill is a masterpiece of harmony and design. Every rock, tree, and timber structure is meticulously placed to reflect traditional aesthetics.

The Pavilion of Absolute Perfection, with its golden hues and red bridge, is the garden’s crown jewel. Stroll past koi ponds, cascading waterfalls, and bonsai displays. The garden is managed by the adjacent Chi Lin Nunnery, and together they offer a peaceful retreat from the city’s chaos.
