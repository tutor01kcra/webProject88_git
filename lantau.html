Lantau Island - Scenic Spots

🧘 Tian Tan Buddha

Towering 34 meters high and weighing over 250 tons, the Tian Tan Buddha is one of the world’s largest seated bronze Buddhas. Located at Ngong Ping on Lantau Island, this serene giant sits atop a lotus throne, surrounded by six devas offering gifts symbolizing the virtues of enlightenment.

Visitors climb 268 steps to reach the statue’s base, where sweeping views of the South China Sea and lush mountains await. Inside, three halls house Buddhist relics and exhibits. The nearby Po Lin Monastery offers a peaceful retreat and delicious vegetarian meals. Whether you arrive via the scenic Ngong Ping 360 cable car or by mountain trail, the journey is as awe-inspiring as the destination.

🐟 Tai O Fishing Village

Known as the “Venice of the East,” Tai O is a living relic of Hong Kong’s fishing heritage. Wooden stilt houses line the waterways, home to the Tanka people who’ve lived here for generations. Wander through narrow alleys filled with dried seafood stalls, shrimp paste vendors, and traditional snacks.

Hop on a boat tour to explore the canals and, if you're lucky, spot the elusive pink dolphins offshore. Don’t miss the Tai O Heritage Hotel, a restored colonial police station perched on a hill. Tai O is a blend of rustic charm, cultural depth, and coastal beauty.

🌊 Cheung Sha Beach

Stretching over 3 kilometers, Cheung Sha Beach is Hong Kong’s longest and one of its most pristine. Divided into Upper and Lower sections, it offers something for everyone—from tranquil sunbathing to water sports like kayaking and paddleboarding.

Lower Cheung Sha is dotted with beachfront cafés and seafood restaurants, perfect for a lazy lunch with ocean views. Upper Cheung Sha, backed by lush hills, is quieter and ideal for peaceful walks or a sunset picnic. It’s a slice of tropical paradise just a bus ride away from the city.
